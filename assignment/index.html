HOW TO PROTECT YOURSELF

There is currently no vaccine to prevent coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19). The best way to prevent illness is to avoid being exposed to this virus. Know the symptoms and self assess regularly.WHO also encourages the use of masks and sanitizers with a minimum of 60% alcohol...

WHO ARE AT RISK

Everyone is equally likely to catch the coronavirus, however, older adults and people who have severe underlying medical conditions like heart or lung disease or diabetes seem to be at higher risk for developing serious complications from COVID-19 illness...

WHAT TO DO IF YOU ARE SICK

If you have a fever, cough or other symptoms, you might have COVID-19. Most people have mild illness and are able to recover at home. If you think you may have been exposed to COVID-19, contact your healthcare provider or your local helpline for assistance.

OTHER IMPORTANT INFORMATION

TRAVEL DURING COVID

Travel increases your chance of getting and spreading COVID-19. Staying home is the best way to protect yourself and others from COVID-19. However, if the travel is essential, read the guidelines to protect yourself and your close ones from COVID while you are travelling.

MENTAL WELL BEING

The coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19) pandemic may be stressful for people. Fear and anxiety about a new disease and what could happen can be overwhelming and cause strong emotions in adults and children.

VACCINE DEVELOPMENT

Researchers around the world are racing to develop a vaccine against Covid-19, with more than 140 candidate vaccines now tracked by the World Health Organization (WHO). Scientists are hoping to develop a coronavirus vaccine within 12 to 18 months.
